ALUMNI ASSOCIATION AND MEETS | USICT

Our present graduates and post graduate would assuredly maintain the same level of performance and take their recruiting organizations to new heights in their respective fields. We highly value our partnership with alumni of Gautam Buddha University and remain committed to provide a productive and positive experience. We heartily invite the alumni organizations and graduating students to find the best match between their needs and capabilities. We are available for any requisite assistance & would ensure a positive & productive experience. Feedback & queries would be welcome and thoroughly entertained and suggestions would be appreciated.

USICT CLUBS | USICT


  • TECHNO CULTURAL CLUB

    • Programming Club

    The Club has an artistic, inventive, motivating, and cooperative platform to provide programmers to communicate and learn from one another through a wide range of programs. It aims at identifying and developing innovative skills with assured guidance and proper mentoring to make them future innovators, technology players, and entrepreneurs. Programming Club is a bunch of passionate individuals in coding, cybersecurity, web design, and other programming topics. We host programming events, information sessions, Seize the Statue competitions, and other events connected to innovation (web development, open standards, and game/app innovation), as well as programming campaigns and webinars. Webinar sessions on analytics, web piracy, and innovation, among other topics, are held regularly. In addition, participants can use the Computer Programming Club to hone their programming abilities in preparation for competition in various programming contests. Each learner has the option of working alone or in a group. They can also pick the program they want to build for submission to programming contests. Computer programming, website architecture, and network management are just a few of our specialties. Our objective is to improve field skills through team projects, tournaments, and guest lecturers in the profession.

    Robotics Clubs

    Technologies have proven a conduit for engaging the high achievers and students who may not be tech-savvy otherwise until they can see an attractive, real-life application for the expertise they have gained. A robotic club is a meeting of students who want to study and develop robots. Students design and construct their robots from the ground up and must program robots for both the autonomous and user-controlled segments of the competition. Students work in groups, speed, curiosity, and great excitement are competitions, which isan excellent technique to get engineering design started. Students may assemble and operate their bots from scratch and program them for both the unassisted and user-controlled portions of the contest. The foundations of technical architecture and computer programming are taught to students. Students are divided into age and experience categories. Competitions are fast-paced, intense, and thrilling. It's a fantastic method to get your foot in the door of engineering design.

    Audio-Video Club

    The Audio-Visual Club consists of students interested in participating in stage crews or the technical team for events. Students joining the club can choose an area of interest, such as the stage crew or the technical team. A short training is offered once a student has decided where he wants to be placed.

    Photography Club

    The Photography Club is a place where like-minded individuals may meet and share information about the art and science of photography. Students are encouraged to study light and are given writing advice by the club. The group organizes events that allow members to study photography, fully utilize their cameras, and become more aware of their environment. The club "focuses" on the interaction between professionals and students, as well as boosting peer photography learning and creating an environment where memories may be captured forever. The photographic club promotes photography amongst students by connecting people keen to assist one other in taking better images together. In addition, picture knowledge and skills are developed, promoted, and improved. The club holds various talks, demonstrations, and contests. We make it a point to encourage anybody who has a passion for cinematography regardless of their level of expertise.

    Poster Making Club

    The club aims to develop the students artistically and socially to leave the university as independent, confident, cooperative, responsible, and creative young adults with a lifelong interest and ability to learn the finesse of life. Although visuals massages leave the best impression and long-lasting too, poster making is an art to be learned for the welfare of society.The most significant benefit of producing a poster is that it promotes camaraderie and comprehension and imagination, comprehensive research, and reading. In addition, it allows students to acquire via doing, which strengthens their understanding.

    Quiz Club

    Quiz Club offers a new and exciting approach to motivate and inspire youngsters and provide them the opportunity to honor their success as part of a broader competition. The club's primary goal is to foster and spread a quizzing ethos inside the university. In addition, the club's goal is to elicit and strengthen intrinsic logical thinking abilities used in quizzing in the hope to cater to the participants' and club members' general artistic and cognitive growth.

    Social Media Club

    The social media club is a student-centric club with objectives like Creation, moderation, and updating of content related to the various social, cultural, academic, and club-related events. The club also fosters the Industry-University linkage by posting the content related to guest lectures by corporate delegates, plant visits, and industrial visits organized by the University. Besides, the club, in general, is helping in generating feedback about the various curricular, co-curricular, extracurricular, summer internship, and placement-related activities that act as an essential input for planning.

    Eco Kart Club

    Eco Kart club supports Eco-Promoted Green Engineering Concept. The club's primary goal is to encourage creative ideas in fuel efficiency, i.e. electrically propelled vehicles, to minimize the use of organic fuel. This helps to preserve organic fuel and helps kids work on pollution rides and fuel for the future.

    Gaming Club

    Students share their passion for gaming in this club. The aim is to motivate new ideas for gaming to encourage youngsters to a new vision in technology. The club organizes internal competitions. In addition, the club is constantly updated with the latest trends in gaming. The group aims to promote gaming as a more socially acceptable form of relaxation and amusement and investigate, play, and debate interactive media, including video games, role-playing games, and board games. We make every effort to arrange events to encourage member participation, but we also urge members to arrange their unofficial gatherings. However, we recognize that many members may not have the time to participate in some events physically, so we offer a group chat for those who would rather play with others in the comfort of their own homes or play whenever they have the opportunity.

    Ethical Hacking Club

    The club motivates the Ethical hacking concept, which involves an authorized attempt to gain unauthorized access to a computer system, application, or data. Also known as “white hats,” ethical hackers are security experts that perform these assessments. Ethical hackers use their knowledge to secure and improve the technology of an organization. This field has significant opportunities for students; they can provide essential service to the organizations by looking for vulnerabilities that can lead to a security breach.

    Design Club

    The club motivates new ideas and gives a platform to young minds to show creativity; learning technology and applying it with creativity is a crucial feature of this club. Design Club is for students who want to learn how to use design thinking and innovation in their jobs and personal life. The designers' group seeks to foster creativity while also improving technical knowledge and productivity among college students. It accomplishes this by hosting various interactive seminars and hands-on enjoyable teaching sessions through its multiple technical clubs. It even allows users to develop goods and leads them through obtaining patents for their creations. In addition, the Design Club is a one-stop-shop for anything related to design. The club members explore a diverse array of themes, from autonomous cars to simple toys, from coding challenges to tackling nerve environmental concerns with mechanical contraptions, and so on, all of which help bring innovation in any way possible.

    App Development Club

    App Development is the requirement of every sector, every business, private, or government. In this club students learn, the latest tools and tech to develop apps. In addition, students perform minor projects and tasks. Members of the App Development Club get the chance to study, practice, and cooperate on app development. The club concentrates on creating user-facing applications for iOS, Android, and the web. Still, it is willing to collaborate with any other platform that allows for this type of software. We want to build a community of programmers interested in assisting one another in learning and improving their programming skills, and we welcome anybody, regardless of ability level.

  • ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE STUDY CIRCLE

    • Artificial intelligence is a constellation of several diverse technologies that operate together to make machines understand, act, and learn with human intelligence levels. Perhaps that is why the concept of everybody's artificial intelligence looks different: AI is not simply one thing.

    Vision

    The Study Circle Motivates the students interested in working in the field of Artificial intelligence. Deep understanding of AI and how it works, the available applications are also introduced to the students. A brief of the latest trends in AI is provided through discussion lectures and meetings. Interested students are guided in their projects and research.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Arun Solanki

  • BLOCK CHAIN STUDY CIRCLE

    • Blockchain technology is a framework that holds transactional block recorded publicly into a network connected by peer-to-peer nodes in a number of data base known as the 'chain.' Every transaction in this directory is approved by the digital signature of the owner, authenticates the transaction and protects it from being disturbed. The information included in the digital ledger is therefore extremely safe.

    Vision

    The circle helps to learn about bitcoin mining, how does blockchain work, the process of transactions, and other main elements of blockchain technology like Blockchain Wallet, Cryptocurrency, and Ethereum. In addition, students get to learn the trending applications of blockchain, a smart contract, and the applications of blockchain experts.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Pradeep Tomar

  • BIG DATA AND ANALYTICS STUDY CIRCLE

    • Big Data Analytics basically includes gathering data from multiple sources to be available for analysts to consume and eventually to supply the company's data products. Big data analysis focuses on the process of turning huge quantities of unstructured raw data collected from many sources into a valuable data product for organisations.

    Vision

    The Study Circle helps in better understanding of Big Data Analytics and the role of a Data Analyst. In addition, students are motivated to find new ideas and applications of Big Data Analytics.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Anurag Singh Baghel

  • BIOINFORMATICS STUDY CIRCLE

    • Bioinformatics is the science in which vast volumes of biological information are stored, collected, and analysed. There are many diverse professionals in a highly multidisciplinary subject, including biologists, scientists of molecular life, computer scientists and mathematics.

    Vision

    The Study Circle motivates students interested in bioinformatics. The main emphasis is on the creation of software tools for the storage, management, interpretation, and analysis of genome, proteome, transcriptome, and metabolome data. In primary bioinformatic studies, information from DNA, protein sequences and the structure and function of protein are integrated. Experts assist students have a detailed understanding of the topic.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Pradeep Tomar

  • COGNITIVE COMPUTING STUDY CIRCLE

    • In computerized models the objective of cognitive computing is to replicate human thinking processes. The computer can imitate the way the human brain functions by using self-learning algorithms that employ data mining, pattern recognition, and natural language processing.

    Vision

    The aim of the cognitive computing study circle is to encourage students to develop new computer frameworks to handle complex issues without continual human involvement. In addition, comprehensive conceptual assistance is offered to students to apply cognitive function computing in commercial and wide-ranging applications.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Arun Solanki

Businesses migrate to a more integrated end-to-end digital transformation using the cloud in their core from a piece-meal strategy. Tomorrow's victors will be those who will traverse swiftly this transition, decide correctly and activate their capacities with appropriate partners.

  • IOT AND IIOT STUDY CIRCLE

    • The Internet of Thingen (IoT) is a system of internet-related subjects which, without any human involvement, may gather and send data over a wireless network. There are unlimited personal and business opportunities. The industrial network of things (IIoT), in industrial sectors and applications, refers to the extension and usage of internet of things (IoT). IIoT covers industrial applications, such as robots, medical devices and manufacturing processes defined by software.

    Vision

    This Study Circle helps the interested students search for new possibilities and applications of IoT and IIOT by guiding them through informative lectures, discussions, and meetings. In addition, students are constantly updated with the latest trends and technology in the circle.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Vidushi Sharma

  • MACHINE LEARNING STUDY CIRCLE

    • Machine learning (ML) is a kind of artificial intelligence (AI) which enables software programs to anticipate results more accurately without having to implement them directly. Instead, previous data are used to prevent new results using machine learning algorithms.

    Vision

    The interested students are motivated for new research and application of ML through projects and writing research papers. The experts provide all the conceptual guidance and understanding.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Pradeep Tomar

  • METRO TRAIN RAMS STUDY CIRCLE

    • In Railways, the RAMS Engineering and Management can be applied to all Railway Systems and plays a vital role at different product life cycle stages starting from conceptual design, manufacturing, operation, maintenance, and replacements to disposal. It systematically designs, studies failure processes, finds out the root causes, suggests improvements, and quantifies the product performance throughout its mission time compared to the conventional engineering practices, mainly focusing on designing systems for specific functional requirements.

    Vision

    This study circle aims to provide students with the knowledge of Metro Train RAMS and find opportunities through product-based projects. In addition, the circle helps students to understand the concept andthe opportunity to work in running projects with experts.

    Study Circle Coordinator:Dr. Rajesh Mishra

  • NANOTECHNOLOGY STUDY CIRCLE

    • Neuro devices are any instruments that are utilized for brain activity monitoring or regulation. At now some of the treatments for Parkinson's disease are available for clinical usage. Deep brain stimulants (DBS) are the most popular neuro technologies for the electrical activation of inactive regions. Moreover, neurodiagnostic technicians conduct several tests that identify brain and nervous system abnormalities and sleep disorders. The data collected from these tests can assist to detect epilepsy, convulsive conditions, strokes, brain degenerative conditions, etc.

    Vision

    The study circle aims to introduce students to new technologies and the latest inventions, and with their interest, they can further work in this area.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Prof. Sanjay Kumar Sharma

    • RF and Microwave Engineering is a part of the subcategory of Electrical Engineering. The RF and Microwave Engineering specialists design, construct and operate the devices transmitting or receiving radio waves. The objects in this field include radios, mobile phones, wireless equipment, and some other objects.

    Vision

    The Study Circle helps in better understanding of RF and Microwave Engineering through lecture discussions and meetings. As a result, students are motivated to find new ideas and applications in the field.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. R. B. Singh

  • ROBOTICS STUDY CIRCLE

    • Robotics is the convergence of science, technology and engineering that generates robotic machinery that replaces or replicates human operations. Robots develop intellectual and mechanical skills, which in the future will not exclude the potential of an R2-D2-like machine.

    Vision

    A study circle for robotics is a gathering of students who would want to learn about robots and work with them. In the circle, students are driven by projects and contests such as teams of students who design and construct a robot to participate in a series of particular tasks.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Anurag Singh Baghel

  • SENSOR AND ADHOC NETWORK STUDY CIRCLE

    • Wireless ad hoc networks may be used and inter-connected wirelessly by sensors, videos, instruments and other devices for monitoring, doctor alerting, and sensing of these data at fusion points fast to help save lives.

    Vision

    The Study Circle helps increase the understanding of students in Sensor and ad hoc networks. Robustness, adaptability and mobility are the advantages of the system. Adhoc network is able toanalyze the performance optimisation environment. This generally needs the positioning capacity and memory of the network node to remember local geographical circumstances. Experts assist pupils to comprehend the topics.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Vimlesh Kumar Ray

  • SYSTEM ON CHIP (VLSI AND OPTICAL) STUDY CIRCLE

    • A Chip, sometimes called a software system, is mainly a single platform integrated circuit or IC and incorporates a complete electrical or computer system. It is a complete system on a single chip, exactly as its name indicates. The input and output pports, internal memory, and analog input and output blocks are part of those components that the SoC usually tries to include inside itself. It is able to perform different duties, such signal processing, wireless communication, artificial intelligence and more, depending on the type of system that has been reduced to a chip size.

    Vision

    The study circle aims to introduce students to new technologies and the latest invention, and with their interest, they can further work in this area.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Priyanka Goel

  • VLSI DESIGN STUDY CIRCLE

    • Silicon CMOS technology has over the last few years become the dominant technique for manufacturing reasonably high performance and cost efficient VLSI circuits. The revolutionary nature and the quick increase in the number of transistors integrated into the circuit on one single chip are the reasons for these advances. We offer the idea of integrated MOS circuits and VHDL and Verilog language coding.

    Vision

    This study cycle is intended for students interested in VLSI technology. The VHDL and Verilog programs are provided to the learners.VLSI conceptual understanding through lecture discussions and meetings.

    Study Circle Coordinator: Dr. Navaid Z. Rizvi
